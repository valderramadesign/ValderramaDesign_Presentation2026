Project One

Optimizing Loan Application
in PayPal Checkout

Reducing friction in the Pay in 4 application flow to drive conversion and customer confidence.

Impact & Problem

A 3-step application
was costing us
conversions

Too many steps in the loan application created confusion and drop-off at critical moments in checkout.

3 steps

Original application flow

High

Drop-off at step 2

Pay in 4

Flagship BNPL product

Relevance

Checkout is the
moment of truth

Every extra step in checkout is an opportunity for the customer to abandon. In lending, trust and clarity are everything.

Scope

Process modification for loan applications

Creating consistent messaging across flows

Research-driven design iterations

Competitor analysis & user testing

Research Phase

Understanding the
Landscape

We conducted general user research and competitive analysis to identify friction points and industry best practices in BNPL checkout flows.

General Research

User interviews, analytics review, drop-off analysis

Competitor Analysis

Benchmarked Affirm, Klarna, Afterpay flows

Key Learnings

What Research Revealed

01

Simplicity wins

Users abandon when they can't predict how many steps remain

02

Messaging inconsistency erodes trust

Different language across steps made users question legitimacy

03

Competitors set expectations

One-step approvals from competitors became the benchmark

The Solution

From 3 Steps to 1 Step

We consolidated the entire loan application into a single, confidence-building step — reducing cognitive load and increasing completion rates.

Before

Step 1 — Personal Info
Step 2 — Verification
Step 3 — Confirmation

After

Single-Step Application

Impact & Challenge

no one could find

Legacy Flow

Monthly Invoicing ← buried here

Optimization

Two Levers for Growth

Conversion

Learnings & Next Steps
